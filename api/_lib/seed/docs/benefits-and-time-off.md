# Benefits & Time Off (US Full-Time)

## Scope

**This policy applies only to US-based, full-time employees of Northwind.** It describes the benefits, paid time off, and leave programs available to that group. If you are a full-time employee based in the United States, everything below applies to you.

**This policy does not cover non-US employees, contractors, consultants, or interns.** Benefits and time-off arrangements for those groups are governed by separate documents or are handled case by case; please contact HR (hr@northwind.example) for guidance if you fall into one of those categories. Nothing here should be read as a statement of policy for anyone other than US-based full-time employees.

Northwind reserves the right to modify, amend, or discontinue any benefit or program described here at any time, subject to applicable law and the terms of the governing plan documents. Where this summary and an official plan document conflict, the plan document controls.

## Health, Dental & Vision

Northwind offers comprehensive medical, dental, and vision coverage to US full-time employees and their eligible dependents (spouse, domestic partner, and children).

- **Medical:** Choose from a PPO plan and a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) paired with a Health Savings Account (HSA). Northwind contributes to the HSA for employees enrolled in the HDHP.
- **Dental:** A single dental PPO plan covering preventive, basic, and major services, plus orthodontia for dependent children.
- **Vision:** A vision plan covering annual exams, lenses, frames, and contact lens allowances.

Northwind pays the majority of the premium for employee-only coverage and a substantial share of dependent premiums. Your share is deducted pre-tax from each paycheck.

### Enrollment Window

- **New hires** must enroll within **30 days** of their start date. Coverage is effective on the first of the month following your start date (or on your start date where the plan allows).
- **Open enrollment** runs each year in **November**, with elections effective January 1.
- Outside those windows, you may only change elections after a **qualifying life event** (marriage, birth or adoption, loss of other coverage, etc.), and you must submit the change within **30 days** of the event.

If you do not enroll during your new-hire window, you will have no coverage until the next open enrollment or a qualifying life event.

## 401(k) Retirement Plan

All US full-time employees are eligible to participate in the Northwind 401(k) plan beginning on their first day. You may contribute on a pre-tax (traditional) or Roth basis, up to the annual IRS limit.

- **Company match:** Northwind matches **100% of the first 4%** of eligible pay you contribute each period. To receive the full match, contribute at least 4% of your pay.
- **Vesting:** Your own contributions are always 100% vested. Company matching contributions vest immediately.
- Enrollment and contribution changes are made through the plan administrator's portal, linked from the HRIS.

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

Northwind operates a **flexible ("unlimited") PTO** policy for US full-time employees. There is no fixed annual accrual; instead, we trust you to take the time you need to rest and recharge while meeting your commitments.

- We set a **minimum expectation of 15 days** of PTO per year. Managers are expected to encourage their teams to meet or exceed this minimum.
- Because PTO is not accrued, it is **not banked and not paid out** at termination.
- PTO covers vacation and personal days. Sick leave and company holidays are tracked separately (see below).
- Extended absences (generally more than 10 consecutive business days) should be planned with your manager and, where applicable, coordinated with the leave programs below.

## Company Holidays

Northwind observes the following **11 paid company holidays** each year for US full-time employees:

- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Juneteenth National Independence Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Columbus Day
- Veterans Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Christmas Day

When a holiday falls on a weekend, Northwind designates the nearest weekday as the observed day off and communicates the date in advance. Company holidays do not count against your PTO.

## Sick Leave

Sick leave is separate from PTO. US full-time employees receive paid sick leave to use for their own illness, medical appointments, or to care for an ill family member.

- Northwind provides **up to 10 paid sick days per calendar year**, or the amount required by applicable state or local law, whichever is greater.
- Sick leave does not carry over year to year unless required by law, and it is not paid out at termination.
- For absences of more than **3 consecutive days**, HR may request documentation from a healthcare provider.
- If a health condition extends beyond available sick leave, contact HR about disability and leave options.

## Parental Leave

Northwind provides paid parental leave to US full-time employees following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child.

- **Primary caregiver:** **16 weeks** of paid leave.
- **Secondary caregiver:** **8 weeks** of paid leave.

Parental leave must generally be taken within **12 months** of the child's arrival and may be taken continuously or, with manager approval, intermittently. Parental leave runs concurrently with any leave you are entitled to under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) or applicable state leave laws. Your health, dental, and vision coverage continues during parental leave on the same terms as active employment.

## How to Request Time Off

All time off — PTO, sick leave, and parental leave — is requested and tracked in the **HRIS** (linked from the intranet).

1. Log in to the HRIS and open **Time Off > New Request**.
2. Select the type of leave (PTO, sick, parental) and the dates.
3. Submit the request to route it to your manager.
4. Your **manager reviews and approves** the request. You'll receive a confirmation in the HRIS once it's approved.

Please request planned PTO **at least two weeks in advance** where possible so your team can plan around your absence. Sick leave can be logged retroactively when an unexpected illness prevents advance notice. For parental leave, notify your manager and HR as early as practicable — ideally at least **30 days** before your intended start date — so we can coordinate coverage and paperwork.

For any questions about eligibility or how a benefit applies to your situation, contact HR at hr@northwind.example.
