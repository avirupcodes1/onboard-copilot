# Sales Playbook

Welcome to the Northwind sales team. This playbook is the single source of truth for how we sell. It exists so that a new rep and a tenured rep run the same motion, forecast off the same signals, and hand customers off cleanly. Read it, keep it open in your first quarter, and revisit it whenever a deal feels stuck.

## Our Sales Process

Every opportunity moves through seven stages. A deal does not advance until the exit criteria for its current stage are met. Skipping stages is the most common cause of slipped deals and bad forecasts.

- **Prospect** — Identify and reach out to accounts that fit our Ideal Customer Profile (below). Use a mix of outbound email, LinkedIn, referrals, and inbound leads routed from marketing. Exit: you have a live conversation booked.
- **Qualify (BANT)** — Confirm the deal is real before you invest time. Exit: all four BANT criteria are documented in Salesforce.
  - **Budget** — Is money allocated, or is there a realistic path to funding this fiscal year?
  - **Authority** — Who signs? Who influences? Map the buying committee, not just your champion.
  - **Need** — What business problem are they solving, and what happens if they do nothing?
  - **Timeline** — Is there a compelling event (renewal, audit, launch) that creates urgency?
- **Discovery** — Go deep on the customer's workflow, pain, current tooling, and success metrics. Ask open questions and listen more than you talk. Exit: you can articulate their problem in their words and quantify the cost of inaction.
- **Demo** — Deliver a tailored demonstration mapped to what you learned in discovery. Never give a generic feature tour. Exit: the customer confirms the solution addresses their stated needs.
- **Proposal** — Present pricing, scope, and ROI in writing. Tie every line item back to a discovered need. Exit: proposal delivered and reviewed with the economic buyer.
- **Negotiation** — Work through pricing, terms, security review, and legal. Trade concessions; never give one away for free. Exit: verbal agreement on terms.
- **Close** — Signatures collected, order booked, deal marked Closed Won. Exit: signed contract and completed handoff to Customer Success.

## CRM Usage — Salesforce Is Mandatory

If it isn't in Salesforce, it didn't happen. Our forecast, comp, and coaching all run off CRM data, so discipline here is non-negotiable.

- **Log every touch** — Emails, calls, meetings, and demos get an activity record the same day, with notes on what was discussed and the next step.
- **Keep stages current** — Update the opportunity stage as reality changes, not at end of quarter. A stalled deal sitting in "Demo" for six weeks misleads everyone.
- **Maintain accurate close dates and amounts** — Your forecast is only as good as these fields. Adjust them honestly.
- **Document next steps and BANT** — Every open opportunity should show a scheduled next action and the current state of Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline.
- **Clean pipeline weekly** — Before every forecast call, review your deals and close out anything dead. A tidy pipeline is a credible pipeline.

## Quota and Commission

- **Monthly quota** — Each Account Executive carries a monthly bookings quota (typically **$40,000 in new ARR**, set per segment and tenure). Ramping reps get a graduated quota for their first two months.
- **Base plus variable** — Compensation is a 50/50 split between base salary and on-target commission. Commission is paid on booked, signed deals.
- **Standard rate** — You earn a flat commission rate on all bookings up to 100% of quota.
- **Accelerators past quota** — Once you clear 100% of monthly quota, every additional dollar pays at an accelerated rate (**1.5x** from 100–125% of quota, **2x** above 125%). Overachievement is where real earnings live, so keep selling after you hit target.
- **Payout timing** — Commissions are paid the month following the booked deal, subject to the deal remaining in good standing.

## Ideal Customer Profile

Spend your time where we win. Our best-fit customer looks like this:

- **Company size** — 200 to 2,000 employees; large enough to have the problem, small enough to move quickly.
- **Industry** — Mid-market B2B, especially SaaS, professional services, and technology-forward operations teams.
- **Pain** — Manual, error-prone workflows that cost measurable time and money each month.
- **Buyer** — A director- or VP-level owner of operations or revenue who feels the pain personally and controls or influences budget.
- **Red flags** — No budget authority, no compelling event, or a "just researching" posture with no timeline. Disqualify early and move on.

## Objection Handling

Objections are buying signals — they mean the customer is engaged. Acknowledge, ask, then respond.

- **"It's too expensive."** Reframe around ROI and the cost of inaction. Anchor to the value you quantified in discovery, not the sticker price.
- **"We're happy with our current tool."** Explore what's missing. Ask what would have to be true for them to switch, then map to those gaps.
- **"Now isn't a good time."** Surface the compelling event. If there genuinely isn't one, this is a qualification problem — revisit BANT.
- **"I need to talk to my team."** Great — offer to help. Ask who else needs to be involved and propose a group session so you stay in the room.
- **"Send me some information."** Often a soft brush-off. Trade the material for a specific next step: "Happy to — can we book 20 minutes Thursday to walk through it?"

## Handoff to Customer Success

The deal isn't done at signature — a clean handoff protects renewal and expansion.

- **Trigger the handoff at Closed Won** — Complete the internal handoff form in Salesforce with the customer's goals, stakeholders, timeline, and any commitments made during the sale.
- **Run a joint kickoff** — Introduce the CSM warmly on a shared call so the customer feels continuity, not a cold restart.
- **Transfer full context** — Share discovery notes, success metrics, and known risks. Never let the CSM rediscover what you already learned.
- **Stay available** — Remain a friendly point of contact through onboarding, but let CS own the relationship. Your next quota is in the next deal.
